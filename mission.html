DoseGO - Mission Statement

DoseGO's Mission

DoseGO is a public health initiative that restores freedom of movement to people receiving methadone treatment, recognizing mobility, autonomy, and social connection as essential determinants of health. Many individuals decline or disengage from methadone care due to the rigid, daily clinic requirements often described as “liquid handcuffs,” which prevent participation in weddings, funerals, family milestones, work opportunities, and travel. Grounded in harm-reduction principles and informed by research such as the Rat Park study, DoseGO challenges the idea that recovery must come at the cost of a meaningful life. Instead, it reframes methadone treatment as something that should support stability, dignity, and connection. By simplifying travel coordination and reducing logistical and administrative barriers, DoseGO addresses a systemic gap in treatment infrastructure—one that disproportionately affects low-income individuals and those with limited flexibility—so that continuity of care and freedom do not have to be mutually exclusive.

DoseGO's future goals:

DoseGO’s long-term goal is to modernize how methadone clinics and patients coordinate care by strengthening clinic-patient communication, reducing paperwork errors, and increasing transparency throughout the travel dosing process. While the current platform empowers patients to initiate requests with confidence through an itinerary builder and clinic-ready communication scripts, future iterations aim to integrate clinic workflows directly—providing real-time request tracking, approval status updates, dosing confirmations, and cost visibility in one accessible interface. By reducing administrative friction on both sides, DoseGO seeks to normalize mobility within methadone care and encourage broader institutional recognition that patient freedom supports, rather than undermines, recovery. Ultimately, DoseGO envisions a treatment landscape where geographic freedom is not treated as an exception, but as a standard component of compassionate, effective, and equitable public health practice.
