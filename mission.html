dosego.org - Mission Statement

dosego.org's Mission

dosego.org is a public health initiative that restores freedom of movement and travel to people receiving methadone treatment. Many individuals decline or disengage from methadone care due to the rigid, daily clinic requirements often described as “liquid handcuffs,” which present barriers to ability to attend events and travel. dosego.org challenges the idea that recovery must come at the cost of a meaningful life. Instead, it reframes methadone treatment as something that should support stability, dignity, and freedom. By simplifying travel coordination and reducing logistical and administrative barriers, dosego.org addresses a systemic gap in treatment infrastructure. Continuity of care and freedom do not have to be mutually exclusive.

dosego.org's future goals:

dosego.org’s long-term goal is to modernize how methadone clinics and patients coordinate care while traveling by strengthening clinic-patient communication, reducing paperwork errors, and increasing transparency throughout the travel (guest) dosing process. While the current platform empowers patients to initiate requests with confidence through an itinerary builder and clinic-ready communication scripts, future iterations aim to integrate clinic workflows directly (real-time request tracking, approval status updates, dosing confirmations, and cost visibility) in one accessible interface. By reducing administrative friction on both sides, dosego.org seeks to normalize mobility and travel within methadone care and encourage broader institutional recognition that patient freedom supports recovery. Ultimately, dosego.org envisions a treatment landscape where geographic freedom is not treated as an exception, but as a standard component of compassionate, effective, and equitable public health practice.
