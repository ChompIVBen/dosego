dosego.org - Guest Dosing Instructions

1. Make a Home-Clinic Appointment (ASAP)

Bring your itinerary (printed or on your phone). If possible, make this visit 2–4 weeks before travel.

  • Say to staff: “I have a travel itinerary and need guest dosing arranged. Can we start the guest dosing request now?”
  • Ask: “Do you need any forms? Will you contact the clinics on my route? Will I need to pay anything?”
  • Get the name and phone of the staff handling it and sign required consent/forms.
  • Ask for a copy of every signed form for your travel packet.
  • Ask: “Will I pay my home clinic, or will the guest clinics charge me?”
  • If guest clinics collect payment, ask how much per day/dose and which payment methods they accept.
  • If home clinic collects payment, get a receipt.

2. Guest Dosing Confirmation Packet (from your home clinic)

Request a final packet containing at minimum:

  • Confirmation that home clinic authorized guest dosing.
  • List of guest clinics that accepted, with dates/times approved.
  • Contact name & phone at each guest clinic (if available).
  • Any special instructions (arrival time, urine screen rules, etc.).

Reccomended: Get both a paper copy and a digital copy on your phone.

3. Confirm Clinic Hours

Call each guest clinic to confirm dosing start/stop times (many stop by late morning). Also ask about holidays/weekends.

4. Prepare Your Travel Packet

  • Government photo ID
  • Guest Dosing packet (from home clinic, step 2 above)
  • Printed itinerary with clinic addresses & phone numbers
  • Payment method (cash/credit/debit)
  • Any clinic-specific forms
  • Emergency contacts
  • Small notebook to record dosing date/time and staff name

5. At the Guest Clinic

Arrive 15–30 minutes early.

  • Tell front desk: “I’m a guest dosing patient authorized by [home clinic name].” Show your paperwork.
  • If asked to sign an acknowledgement, sign and request a receipt or note the staff name.
  • Record the exact time you received the dose and the staff member’s name in your notebook.

6. If Plans Change or You Miss a Date

Call your home clinic first. Then call the guest clinic only if home clinic instructs.

  • Do not attempt walk-in dosing without authorization.
  • What to say: “Hi, this is [NAME]. My travel plans changed and I need to adjust my dosing date. What should I do to remain authorized?”

7. After the Trip: Confirm Return & Follow Up

  • Let home clinic know you’re back (call or stop by).
  • Bring receipts and paperwork from guest clinics.

Contingency & Safety Reminders

  • Keep phone numbers for home & guest clinics accessible.
  • Carry extra ID and a backup copy of paperwork on your phone.
  • Never skip notifying your home clinic about plan changes.
  • If you feel unwell after dosing, contact the clinic or seek urgent care if it’s an emergency.
